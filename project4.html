Bio-Inspired Blended Wing Body Aircraft

Designed and optimised a bio-inspired Blended Wing Body (BWB) aircraft configuration, focusing on improving aerodynamic efficiency through innovative aircraft geometry and advanced computational analysis. The design process involved creating a highly integrated airframe where the wing and fuselage are blended into a single aerodynamic surface, reducing interference drag and improving overall lift distribution across the aircraft structure. The initial aircraft geometry was developed using SolidWorks, where the blended configuration was modelled to achieve smooth aerodynamic contours and balanced lift generation across the wing-body structure. Special attention was given to the integration of winglets and the aerodynamic transition between the central body and wing surfaces, ensuring an optimal distribution of aerodynamic loads and improved aerodynamic stability. The aerodynamic performance of the aircraft was analysed using Computational Fluid Dynamics (CFD) simulations conducted in ANSYS Fluent. Before performing the main aerodynamic study, the CFD framework was rigorously validated to ensure accuracy and reliability of the simulation results. This validation process included mesh convergence studies, where multiple mesh densities were tested to confirm that the simulation results remained consistent and independent of mesh resolution. In addition, the computational model was benchmarked against established aerodynamic data to verify the credibility of the simulation methodology. Following validation, a parametric aerodynamic study was conducted to analyse the influence of different winglet sweep angles on the aircraft’s aerodynamic performance. Several winglet configurations were tested under identical flow conditions to evaluate their impact on lift generation, drag reduction, and overall aerodynamic efficiency. The analysis identified the winglet configuration that produced the highest lift-to-drag (L/D) ratio, confirming that the optimised geometry significantly enhanced aerodynamic efficiency compared with alternative configurations. The results of the study demonstrated the aerodynamic advantages of the Blended Wing Body configuration, particularly in terms of reduced drag and improved lift efficiency. The optimised design showed strong potential for next-generation aircraft concepts that prioritise fuel efficiency, aerodynamic performance, and innovative structural integration.
