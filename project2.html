SAE Aero Design Competition

Served as Technical Lead for the design and development of a large-scale radio-controlled aircraft for the SAE Aero Design Competition, organised by SAE International. The role involved leading the technical team responsible for aerodynamic analysis, structural evaluation, aircraft configuration development, and CAD modelling throughout the full design cycle. Responsibilities included conceptual aircraft configuration design, airfoil selection, aerodynamic optimisation, weight estimation, and structural reinforcement of critical components to ensure both performance efficiency and structural integrity. The aircraft design process involved extensive use of engineering software tools for aerodynamic simulation, structural analysis, and 3D modelling. Aerodynamic performance was evaluated through computational analysis to estimate lift, drag, and stability characteristics under various flight conditions. Structural evaluation was performed to ensure that the aircraft could safely withstand aerodynamic loads during take-off, cruise, and landing phases while maintaining optimal weight distribution. CAD modelling was used to design and manufacture the aircraft structure, including the wing, fuselage, landing gear system, and propulsion mounting systems. The project achieved national-level recognition at the SAE Aero Design National Championship held in Chennai, India. The team also participated in the SAE Aero Design International Competition held in Orlando, Florida, USA, where a large-scale radio-controlled aircraft with an approximate wingspan of 200 inches was successfully designed, built, and tested. The aircraft demonstrated strong aerodynamic efficiency, structural stability, and payload performance during flight evaluation. Competing against international teams from leading engineering institutions, the project secured 15th place at the international level, highlighting the team’s capabilities in aircraft conceptual design, aerodynamic optimisation, and large-scale prototype development.
